R.A.A.D.S

I developed Robot Anti-Alien Defense Squad solo in under 72hs for the Mini Jam #84. And ooh boy, did I have a good time at it! It scratched my GameDev itch so bad I felt satisfied enough for a year. But I keep coming back to it wanting to do it again, and that's the reason I know this is my true career path. #TODO Image with link

Now that you played the game, let me tell you what I think about it:

What I think is right:

  • The game's design core is clear: "Challenge the player by tying their ammo to their movement"
    • You're no longer able to run and gun like in classic shmups, you're forced to make calculated and very tactical shots.
  • Almost every element of the game ties back into the core.
  • Extensively fine-tuning the game object's values paid off, so its infinite game loop really hooks you in.

    • What I'd do different:

  • Better communicate the main mechanics through UX/UI. As it stands some players may be thrown off by their sudden lack of movement.

    • I developed R.A.A.D.S. using ct.js, which does not provide any Version Control or VC Integration. As such, I have not uploaded my code to GitHub but you may take a look at my code by... {????} .

    Locked Inn

    This is a project that is very dear to me but is yet to be finished. It was initially meant for the Narrative Driven Jam. Due to Covid-19 we stopped working on this game, but after a very eventful year, I can happily announce the team has resumed development and will soon see the light of day!

    Locked Inn is a Murder Mystery styled as a Visual Novel, where you play as the feline successor to the hotel's elevator operator. As such, you must take the guests to their rooms, unavoidably overhearing their conversations. Luckily guests don't mind you picking the order in which you drop them off, so you get to be smart about which conversations will happen, and in so doing, helping you solve the murder of your late owner.

    Here you can witness the first couple of minutes of playtime: #TODO

    Most of the game plays like that. Every night you overhear a situation, choose who to let go, and then listen to the rest unfold. After enough nights you should have enough information to deduce who's behind the awful crime and why.

    Our amazing team consisted of a gameplay designer, an illustrator, a sound designer and me. I handled the game's concept, programming and story, and also the project's coordination. All the effort showcased here was achieved under 10 days, so even though we didn't meet the Jam's deadline I consider it to be a success. And taking into account all the lessons I learned because of it, I can say it was a huge success at that.

    Extreme Ripoff

    Extreme Ripoff is a fully playable game made for my Algorithms and Data Structures II class. It's also an endless source of funtration for anyone playing it (fun AND frustration).

    The art and gameplay design were provided to us, while we were tasked with the technical design and implementation using C++11 and SDL2 as well as to abide by very strict algorithmic optimization guidelines.
    We were a team of 3 and it took us a month to complete with about 15 hours of focused work every week.

    Here you control a holy fire-spewing exorcist, and every time she kills a specific ghost, she gets to relive her previous experience but with one more ghost in the room, which copies your last movements in a loop.
    That's how you end up in a situation like this:
    The main mechanic was shamelessly ripped off from a small indie game called Extreme Exorcism.
    As I mentioned above, our data modeling and algorithms were held to comply with strict time and memory complexities, so the game would be playable on enormous maps with many thousands of ghosts (even running on low-end hardware). This was a challenge, but it was one of the most awesome learning experiences I've had in academia.

    Playing the game: Portability for C++ projects is a bit of a mess, but I'm working on a WebAssembly build so it can be deployed here. On the meantime, you're welcome to look at our code and compile it from source.
